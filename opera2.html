Palace Yard, Westminster

ACT II. Scene.—Palace Yard, Westminster.Westminster Hall, L. Clock tower up, R.C. Private Willis discovered on sentry, R. Moonlight.

LORD MOUNTARARAT.

When Britain really ruled the waves— (In good Queen Bess's time) The House of Peers made no pretence To intellectual eminence, Or scholarship sublime; Yet Britain won her proudest bays In good Queen Bess's glorious days!

CHORUS.

Yes, Britain won, etc. When Wellington thrashed Bonaparte, As every child can tell, The House of Peers, throughout the war, Did nothing in particular, And did it very well: Yet Britain set the world ablaze In good King George's glorious days!

CHORUS.

Yes, Britain set, etc. And while the House of Peers withholds Its legislative hand, And noble statesmen do not itch To interfere with matters which They do not understand, As bright will shine Great Britain's rays As in King George's glorious days!

CHORUS.

As bright will shine, etc.

LEL.

(who has been much attracted by the Peers during this song). Charming persons, are they not?
